Title: Argumentation, Evidence, and Schemes: Approach and Empirical Evaluation

Abstract:
We propose ArgPol, an interactive evidence-based framework for argumentation schemes. We study how evidence can be incorporated into the argumentation process. We demonstrate how belief, disbelief, and uncertainty of the argument can be used as a completeness measure. We show how ArgPol incorporates argumentation schemes, and how it enables agents to improve completeness and potentially reduce uncertainty of an argument by choosing the "right" critical questions to ask. We evaluate the effectiveness of our framework via a human-subject study conducted using a firewall policy modeling scenario.
